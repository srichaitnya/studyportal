Project Apollo

The Apollo program achieved the first human landing on the Moon. Apollo 11 (1969) made Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin the first humans to walk on the lunar surface. Other notable missions include Apollo 13 (emergency mission) and Apollo 17 (last Moon mission).

International Space Station (ISS)

The ISS serves as a microgravity lab for scientific research in astrobiology, physics, material science, and technology development. Experiments help understand Earth's climate and human adaptation to space.

More info: NASA ISS Research

Satellite Missions

NASA satellites monitor Earth and space. Notable missions: Landsat (Earth observation), OCO-2 (CO2 measurement), TESS (exoplanet search), CubeSats for research and technology testing.

More info: NASA Missions

Artemis Program

Artemis aims to return humans to the Moon and prepare for Mars. It will land the first woman and next man on the Moon and establish sustainable lunar exploration.

More info: NASA Artemis

Space Experiments & Research

NASA conducts astrobiology, space medicine, and materials science experiments in microgravity to understand science and develop new technologies.
